Maze Generator

This project is a maze generator achieved with recursive back-tracking algorithm. The maze map is first recorded in a text file, and then the game is put together with the UI from SDL2. Here is how recursive backtracking works in this project:

1. Choose a starting point in the field.

2. Randomly choose a wall at that point and carve a passage through to the adjacent cell, but only if the adjacent cell has not been visited yet. This becomes the new current cell.

3. If all adjacent cells have been visited, back up to the last cell that has uncarved walls and repeat.

4. The algorithm ends when the process has backed all the way up to the starting point.

Average Distance between Random Points

This document discusses the average Euclidean distance between two random points in a unit hypercube. It was written after I completed my first university-level statistics course, and was an application of the knowledge that I obtained from that course. Three math problems were solved. The first two involve evaluating the average distance in lower dimensions (one and two dimensions). The last one involves an approximation of the average distance in higher dimensions. The quality of the approximation was then checked by using MATLAB.

Sangaku Circle Geometry

I consider geometry as a big game of shapes. I love shapes. This is why I am fond of geometry and art. This document discusses three geometry problems with tangent circles, from simple to complicated, all of which were originated from Japan hundreds of years ago. Tangent circles are known as circles in a common plane that intersect in a single point. The key to solving these problems is to make use of the right angles formed by the tangent circles. Most interestingly, the last problem was solved by reducing the problem to a simpler one using conformal mapping in the complex number system.
